    • Study Introduction

    This website demonstrates the variation of four weather attributes with latitude.

    This plot of temperature (below) is useful to point out common similarities and characteristics of the plots of the weather attributes.

  • The x-axis shows latitude, with the South Pole on the left and the North Pole on the left.
  • Though an x-axis range of -90 to 90 would be appropriate, the x-axis limits of these plots were influenced by only the Northern Hemisphere containing cities between 60 and 80 degrees of latitude.
  • This study contains more cities from the Northern Hemisphere.
  • This study contains weather attributes measured on January 5th, 2017; summer in the Southern Hemisphere and winter in the Northern Hemisphere.

    This site attempts to answer these questions and more...

    Cloudiness
    • Are clouds formed from snow at the poles?
    • Does the spin of the earth sling clouds towards the equator?
    • Are clouds sparser at the equator because the earth is widest there?
      Humidity
    • Are the poles high and dry?
    • Do the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn indicate regions of tropical humidity?
    • Does the sun's intense heat dry out the air along the equator?
      Temperature
    • Are the poles heated by the friction of the earth spinning on its axis?
    • Are there any temperatures below 72 degrees between the Tropics of Cancer and Capricorn?
    • Is the temperature uniformly hot along the equator?
      Wind Speed
    • Is the wind calm at the poles, like the eye of a hurricane?
    • If the hemispheres have independent weather systems, are the winds highest at the central latitude of 45 degrees?
    • Are there significant doldrums along the equator?
